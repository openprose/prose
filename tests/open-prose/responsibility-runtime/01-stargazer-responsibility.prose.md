# High-Intent Stargazer Outreach

### Goal

High-intent GitHub stargazers are identified, enriched, and thoughtfully
followed up with using relevant OpenProse sample results.

### Continuity

- New high-intent stargazers should not remain unattended for more than one
  business day.
- Outreach history should prevent duplicate contact unless new evidence changes
  the prospect's fit.
- Stale leads should be revisited when company, role, or repository evidence
  materially changes.

### Criteria

- Qualification includes GitHub activity, likely organization, company context,
  role signals, customers, and plausible operational pain.
- Proposed OpenProse programs are specific to the prospect's observed work.
- Sample results exist before outreach when the prospect appears high intent.
- Outreach asks whether the sample result is useful rather than pushing a
  generic sales message.

### Constraints

- Do not send generic or irrelevant outreach.
- Do not contact the same person repeatedly without new evidence.
- Keep enrichment and sample-generation cost bounded by expected value.
- Escalate uncertainty instead of fabricating company, role, or pain details.

### Fulfillment

Prefer the local `stargazer-outreach` system when present.
